Types of business activity

If you are planning an individual activity, sole tradership is the simplest and cost-efficient method of running a business in Poland.

The two main forms of running a business in Poland are:

  • a company or a partnership
  • a sole tradership

Partnerships and companies are a rather complex form of a business activity. The requirements for their establishment include, for example, a minimum number of founders (at least two), a written instrument (a contract in writing or articles of association in the form of a notarial deed), in most cases – the minimum capital requirement. Due to their complex nature, the establishment of a partnership or a company requires involvement of a lawyer or a notary (or both).

For this reason, this guide will focus on the one form of business activity that an interested person can establish and register independently, without the help of a lawyer – a sole tradership.

A sole tradership is a business activity conducted in a permanent manner by an individual, a natural person. The legal basis for this type of activity is the Act of 6 March 2018 on entrepreneurship law.

To establish a sole tradership, you need to register it with CEIDG.

The only formality required is to fill in a registration form. You can do it on paper or on-line. This guide will help you fill in the required form and submit it to CEIDG.
