Is a bank account required?

When running your business, you can use either a business bank account, or your private bank account. If you choose the second option, though, you must be the only holder of your private account.

There are some transactions that must not be settled in cash. In such cases a bank account is required to effect payment. The detailed regulations are set forth in Article 3(1) of the Act of 13 April 2016 amending the act on personal income tax, the act on corporate tax, and the act freedom of economic activity.

In short, a businessperson must not pay in cash, when:

  • the transaction is with another business
  • a one-off value of the transaction exceeds PLN 15,000, regardless of the number of payments made.

Generally, you will need a bank account to pay your taxes and social security contributions. This requirement does not apply to the so-called micro-businesses. Namely, if in at least one year in the last two years you had employed fewer than 10 persons and your annual net revenue has not exceeded EUR 2 million, you do not need a bank account - you can pay your taxes and social security contributions at the post office.

 

You will need a business bank account (not your private one), if:

  • you are a VAT payer – only business bank account numbers are published in the so-called white list of VAT payers
  • you use the split payment mechanism

In short, it may be convenient to have a business bank account, even when it is not mandatory.

 

  1. Choose a bank that meets your needs.
  2. Consider whether a private bank account will be sufficient for you.
  3. Open a business bank account or a private one.
  4. Enter your bank account details in section 21 of your registration application.
