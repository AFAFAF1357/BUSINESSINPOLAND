Taxation form

Available taxation forms

You should choose one of the three available taxation forms for your business.

When you run a business as a sole trader, you are subject to personal income tax, or PIT.

You can choose one of the following forms of taxation that will apply to your business:

  • general, or tax scale (with tax rates of 12% and 32%)
  • flat-rate tax (the tax rate is 19%)
  • lump-sum tax on registered income.

Tax scale (general rules) (with tax rates of 12% and 32%)

This is the general and default taxation form that will apply to your activity, unless you choose a different form of taxation.

Flat-rate tax

Here you pay tax at the rate of 19% regardless of your income.

Lump-sum tax on registered income

This is a simplified form of taxation, where you pay tax at a specified rate (from 2% to 17%) on your registered income in a given month.

 

Lump sum on registered income taxation is available for specific types of activity only. For example, this taxation form cannot be applied, when you run a pharmacy, trade in currencies, or sell car parts.
