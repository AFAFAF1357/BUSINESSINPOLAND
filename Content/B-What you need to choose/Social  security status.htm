Social security status

When you register your business, you will be automatically registered as a payer of social security contributions. You may be entitled to certain incentives and reliefs in payment of your social security contributions.

Obligations – what contributions you need to pay

When you register your business with CEIDG, you will be automatically registered with the Social Security Office, or ZUS, as a payer of social security and health contributions.

As an entrepreneur, you are obliged to pay the following contributions:

Incentive for new businesses (ulga na start)

When you start a new business, you can benefit from the Incentive for New Businesses. You are eligible for the incentive, if:

  • you start a new business or have not been conducting any for at least 60 months
  • you do not provide services for your former employer (the one for whom you worked in the current or previous calendar year)

If you meet these two conditions, you will be obliged to pay the health security contribution only – the social security contribution and the Labour Fund and the Solidarity Fund payments will be waived. You can use this incentive for 6 full months – a full month means that if you started your business some time mid-month, this month will not be included in the six-month period.

If you want to benefit from this incentive, you need to register with the Social Security Office within 7 days from the commencement of your business activity. You will need to file the ZUS ZZA form. The simplest way is to file the ZUS ZZA form with CEIDG when registering your business. You can do it in section 9.1 of your registration application.

Reduced-base contributions

Reduced-base contributions is another incentive for new businesses. An entrepreneur pays reduced social security contributions for a fixed period.

If you have used the Incentive for New Business and the 6-month period expired, you can pay social security contributions calculated on a reduced base. This entitlement is valid for 24 full months. You are eligible for this relief, if:

  • you have not been conducting a new business in the last 60 months before commencing your business activity
  • you do not run business activities for the benefit of your former employer (the one for whom you worked under an employment agreement in the current or previous calendar year)

The latter condition remains valid during the whole 24-month period when you benefit from the relief. It means that you must not work for your former employer or, if you do, you will lose your entitlement to reduced social security contributions.

The reduced social security contributions are calculated on the reduced base – namely, 30% of the minimum monthly salary. When you start your business activity, you can either apply for Incentive for New Business, or for Reduced-base contributions. If you want to use the Reduced-base contributions after you have used your Incentive for New Business, you need to file the ZUS ZUA form with the Social Security Office. When the 24-month period of the Reduced-base Contribution expires, you will need to file the ZUS ZWUA form with the Social Security Office within 7 days from the end of that period. You will pay standard contributions.
